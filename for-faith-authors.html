The Book You Have Been Carrying

Most faith leaders have a book in them. Few ever get it out.

You have preached the same truths for years, watched them land in people's lives, and thought more than once that they belonged in a book. Perhaps you have a drawer of homilies. Perhaps a season of your ministry that shaped you, in a parish, a mission, a hospital ward, or a prison. Perhaps only a conviction that your years of walking with people have taught you something worth setting down.

The gap is rarely the message. It is the practical business of turning what you carry into a finished book: the writing, the editing, the cover, the setup, and the quiet fear that publishing is either vanity or a maze. We built this studio to close that gap, and to do it in a way that respects both your voice and your calling.

We already produce books for clergy. What Remains by Fr Charles Egbon and The Deeper Hunger by Fr Mark Odion were both made here, from first idea to printed copy. We understand the register, the reverence, and the reader.

How It Works

From a first conversation to a book in your hands.

We can start from a finished manuscript, a folder of homilies, or nothing but an idea and your voice.

01

We listen

A gentle conversation about your ministry, your reader, and the book you carry. No pressure, no pitch. We tell you honestly whether there is a book here and which route fits.

02

We shape it

From interviews, your homilies, or a draft, we build an outline and then the chapters, in your voice. You review every stage. Editors improve writing; they never replace the person behind it.

03

We make it beautiful

A cover designed with reverence and care, interior formatting for paperback and ebook, and every small decision a properly published book needs. Made to sit well in a reader's hands.

04

We publish it

ISBN, setup, and a launch. Direct sales through our own storefront, Amazon if you want it, or your own imprint. The book stays in print, and you keep the rights to everything it becomes.

Questions Clergy Ask

Honest answers, before you commit to anything.

Click any question to expand.

I have a drawer full of homilies. Can that become a book?
Often, yes. Homilies rarely become a book by being stacked together, but they are a rich seam. We read what you have, find the threads that hold, and shape them into reflections that read as a book rather than a collection of talks. Many faith books begin exactly this way.
Is self-publishing a book vanity? I do not want it to look like that.
A well-made book that helps its readers is not vanity, whoever pays for it. What reads as vanity is a book made carelessly. Our whole craft is aimed at the opposite: a book with a real reader in mind, made to a professional standard, that you would be glad to hand to your bishop or your parish.
I cannot justify a large upfront cost. Is there another way?
Yes. This is exactly why the Partnership route exists. We produce the book at no upfront charge, and once it is selling we share revenue until the production cost is recouped, after which the balance flips firmly in your favour. Your book gets made without a bill you cannot meet.
Will the book still sound like me?
That is the point. We work from your own words, your homilies, and interviews in your voice, and you review every stage. If ghostwriting is involved, we start with sample chapters so you can hear the voice before we write the whole book. We protect your voice, including from our own editing.
Do I keep the rights and the royalties?
On the Full Service route you own everything and keep 100% of what the book earns. On Partnership, the revenue share flips firmly in your favour once production is recouped, and the copyright in your words is always yours. We never ask you to sign away your work.
Can it be ready for a jubilee or ordination anniversary?
Yes, and it is one of the most natural reasons to make a book. Tell us the date early and we build the schedule backwards from it. We produced The Deeper Hunger for Fr Mark Odion's Silver Jubilee, so we know how to hold a hard deadline.
Do you work with clergy of other traditions, not only Catholic?
Yes. We have produced Catholic titles, and we work with ministers and leaders across the Christian traditions. Our craft is the book; your tradition and voice are yours. We handle each with care and without imposing a house line.
Do you sign a confidentiality agreement?
Gladly. If your book touches sensitive pastoral ground, or you would simply prefer discretion, we sign a non-disclosure agreement before any work begins.
What is the first step, and does it cost anything?
A conversation, and no, it costs nothing. About thirty minutes by phone or video, or in writing by email if you prefer. We ask about your book and your situation and tell you honestly what would fit, including whether a book is right for you at all.

Start with a conversation, not a commitment.

Tell us a little about the book you carry and where you are on the journey. We will reply within one working day, tell you honestly which route fits, and take it at your pace. If you would rather write than talk, that is welcome too.
